Permutation • Combination • Probability

Introduction

This website helps students understand permutation, combination, and probability through clear explanations and examples.

Permutation

Definition:

Permutation is the arrangement of objects where order matters.

Formula:

P(n) = n!

Example:

Description:

A teacher wants to arrange 4 students (A, B, C, D) in 4 seats.

Concept: Permutation (order matters)

Solution:

4! = 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 24

Final Answer: 24 arrangements

Combination

Definition:

Combination is the selection of objects where order does not matter.

Formula:

nCr = n! / [(n - r)! r!]

Example:

Description:

Choose 3 members from 5 candidates (E, F, G, H, I).

Concept: Combination

Final Answer: 10 possible committees

Probability

Definition:

Probability is the chance that an event will occur.

Formula:

P(E) = favorable outcomes / total outcomes

Example:

Description:

A 6-sided die is rolled. Find probability of even number.

Solution:

Total outcomes = 6

Favorable outcomes = 3 (2, 4, 6)

P = 3/6 = 1/2 = 0.5

Final Answer: 1/2 (50%)

Step-by-Step Summary

Permutation: 4! = 24

Combination: 10 ways

Probability: 1/2 or 50%
