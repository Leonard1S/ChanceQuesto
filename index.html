Introduction

This website is created to help students understand the basic concepts of permutation, combination and probability in simple and engaging way. These topics are important in mathemathics because they teach us how to count, organize, and predict outcomes in diffrent situation.

Through clear explanations and real-life examples, this project shows how these concepts are used in everyday life—such as arranging people, choosing groups, and calculating chances in games or events.

Permutation

Permutation – Arranging Students in Seats

Description:

A teacher wants to arrange 4 students (A, B, C, D) in 4 seats. The order matters.

Concept Identification:

Permutation (order matters)

Solution:

P(n)=n!

Full Solution:

4!=4x3x2x1=24

Final Answer:

There are 24 different arrangements.

Combination

Combination – Selecting Committee Members

Description:

Choose 3 members from 5 candidates (E, F, G, H, I). Order does not matter.

Concept Identification:

Combination

Full Solution:

nCr = n! ——— (n-r)!r!

Final Answer:

There are 10 possible committees.

Probability

Probability – Rolling a Dice

Description:

A fair 6-sided die is rolled. What is the probability of getting an even number (2, 4, or 6)?

Concept Identification:

Probability (chance of an event happening)

P= Favorable outcomes/total outcomes

Full Solution:

Total outcomes = 6 → (1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6)

Favorable outcomes = 3 → (2, 4, 6)

P(even) = 3/6

Simplify:

= 1/2

Convert to decimal:

= 0.5

Final Answer:

The probability is 1/2, 0.5, or 50%.
