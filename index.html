Permutation • Combination • Probability

Introduction

Mathematics is not only about numbers and formulas—it is also about understanding patterns, making decisions, and solving real-life problems. This website focuses on three important concepts in mathematics: permutation, combination, and probability. These topics help us determine how things can be arranged, how groups can be formed, and how likely certain outcomes are to happen. Permutation deals with situations where the order of objects matters, such as arranging students in seats or organizing items in a specific sequence. Combination, on the other hand, focuses on selecting items where the order does not matter, like choosing members for a group or committee. Probability helps us measure the likelihood of an event occurring, such as predicting the result of a game or chance-based activity. In this website, these concepts are explained using clear definitions, step-by-step solutions, and real-life examples. By connecting mathematical ideas to everyday situations, learners can better understand how these topics are useful beyond the classroom. The goal is to make complex ideas easier to understand and more relatable for students.

Permutation

Permutation – Arranging Students in Seats

Description:

A teacher wants to arrange 4 students (A, B, C, D) in 4 seats. The order matters.

Concept Identification:

Permutation (order matters)

P(n)=n!

Full Solution:

4!=4x3x2x1=24

Final Answer:

There are 24 different arrangements.

Combination

Combination – Selecting Committee Members

Description:

Choose 3 members from 5 candidates (E, F, G, H, I). Order does not matter.

Concept Identification:

Combination

Full Solution:

nCr = n! ——— (n-r)!r!

Final Answer:

There are 10 possible committees.

Probability

Probability – Rolling a Dice

Description:

A fair 6-sided die is rolled. What is the probability of getting an even number (2, 4, or 6)?

Concept Identification:

Probability (chance of an event happening)

P= Favorable outcomes/total outcomes

Full Solution:

Total outcomes = 6 → (1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6)

Favorable outcomes = 3 → (2, 4, 6)

P(even) = 3/6

Simplify:

= 1/2

Convert to decimal:

= 0.5

Final Answer:

The probability is 1/2, 0.5, or 50%.

About

Purpose of the Project

This project was created to serve as an interactive and educational resource for students who are learning about permutation, combination, and probability. Many students find these topics challenging because they involve different formulas and ways of thinking. This website aims to simplify those difficulties by presenting the lessons in a clear, organized, and engaging way. One of the main purposes of this project is to help students clearly distinguish between permutation and combination, as these two concepts are often confused. By providing examples and guided solutions, learners can better identify when to use each formula. In addition, the project highlights the importance of probability in everyday life, showing how it can be used to make predictions and informed decisions. Another goal of this website is to improve students’ problem-solving and critical thinking skills. By working through the examples and understanding the steps involved, learners can develop confidence in solving similar problems on their own. The project also encourages independent learning by allowing users to explore the content at their own pace. Overall, this website was created to make mathematics more accessible, practical, and enjoyable. It demonstrates that math is not just a subject in school, but a useful tool that can be applied in real-life situations such as planning, organizing, and decision-making. Through this project, students are given the opportunity to see the relevance of mathematics and appreciate its importance in everyday life.
