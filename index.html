Permutation • Combination • Probability

Introduction

Mathematics is not only about numbers and formulas—it is also about understanding patterns, making decisions, and solving real-life problems. This website focuses on three important concepts in mathematics: permutation, combination, and probability. These topics help us determine how things can be arranged, how groups can be formed, and how likely certain outcomes are to happen.

In the realm of mathematics, particularly in probability and statistics, several key concepts help us understand and analyze uncertain events.

Combination and permutation are two fundamental counting techniques. A combination is a way to select items from a larger group where the order of selection doesn't matter. On the other hand, a permutation is an arrangement of items where order is crucial.

Factorial, denoted by an exclamation mark (!), is a mathematical operation that involves multiplying a number by all positive integers below it, playing a vital role in calculating permutations and combinations.

When analyzing events, the sample space represents all possible outcomes, while an event is a specific outcome or set of outcomes within that space. Favorable outcomes are those that align with the event of interest.

Probability, a measure of likelihood, comes in two forms: experimental and theoretical. Experimental probability is derived from actual trials or experiments, whereas theoretical probability is calculated based on the number of favorable outcomes divided by the total outcomes in the sample space.

Understanding these concepts provides a solid foundation for tackling problems in probability, statistics, and beyond.

Permutation

Permutation – Arranging Students in Seats

Description:

A teacher wants to arrange 4 students (A, B, C, D) in 4 seats. The order matters.

Concept Identification:

Permutation (order matters)

P(n)=n!

Full Solution:

4!=4x3x2x1=24

Final Answer:

There are 24 different arrangements.

Combination

Probability

Probability – Rolling a Dice

Description:

A fair 6-sided die is rolled. What is the probability of getting an even number (2, 4, or 6)?

Concept Identification:

Probability (chance of an event happening)

P= Favorable outcomes/total outcomes

Full Solution:

Total outcomes = 6 → (1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6)

Favorable outcomes = 3 → (2, 4, 6)

P(even) = 3/6

Simplify:

= 1/2

Convert to decimal:

= 0.5

Final Answer:

The probability is 1/2, 0.5, or 50%.

About

Purpose of the Project

This project was created to serve as an interactive and educational resource for students who are learning about permutation, combination, and probability. Many students find these topics challenging because they involve different formulas and ways of thinking. This website aims to simplify those difficulties by presenting the lessons in a clear, organized, and engaging way.

One of the main purposes of this project is to help students clearly distinguish between permutation and combination, as these two concepts are often confused. By providing examples and guided solutions, learners can better identify when to use each formula. In addition, the project highlights the importance of probability in everyday life, showing how it can be used to make predictions and informed decisions.

Another goal of this website is to improve students’ problem-solving and critical thinking skills. By working through the examples and understanding the steps involved, learners can develop confidence in solving similar problems on their own. The project also encourages independent learning by allowing users to explore the content at their own pace.

Overall, this website was created to make mathematics more accessible, practical, and enjoyable. It demonstrates that math is not just a subject in school, but a useful tool that can be applied in real-life situations such as planning, organizing, and decision-making. Through this project, students are given the opportunity to see the relevance of mathematics and appreciate its importance in everyday life.
