Permutation • Combination • Probability

Introduction

This website is part of the ChanceQuest Digital Guidebook project. It helps Grade 10 students understand permutation, combination, and probability.

The goal is to provide a clear and engaging learning experience using real-life examples and simple explanations.

Educational Content

Definitions:

  • Permutation – arrangement where order matters
  • Combination – selection where order does not matter
  • Probability – chance of an event occurring

Key Terms:

  • Factorial (n!)
  • Sample Space
  • Event
  • Favorable Outcomes
  • Experimental vs Theoretical Probability

Formulas:

nPr = n! / (n - r)!

nCr = n! / [r!(n - r)!]

P(E) = favorable outcomes / total outcomes

Examples

Permutation:

Arrange 3 students → 3! = 6 ways

Combination:

Choose 2 from 3 → 3C2 = 3 ways

Probability:

Coin flip → 1/2 chance of heads
